5. Norm dynamics — the smoking gun

This figure is the diagnostic key. The exact TDVP equation $i\,\dot z_\alpha = C^{-1}_{\alpha\beta}\,g_\beta$ preserves $\langle\psi|\psi\rangle$ identically when $C$ is invertible (because then $\dot\psi$ is exactly orthogonal to $\psi$ in the Hilbert-space sense). The fact that ECG norm drifts means the regularised solve $\dot z = (C + \lambda I)^{-1} g$ (with the SVD $rcond$ cutoff at $10^{-4}$ of $\sigma_\text{max}$) is dropping motion components that are NOT norm-preserving.

Inspection of $\text{cond}(C)$ during the run gives $\text{cond}(C) \approx 3.5\times 10^{11}$, so SVD truncates singular values below $\sigma_\text{max}\cdot 10^{-4} \sim 3.5\times 10^7$, which on a matrix of operator norm $\sim 1$ means many directions of motion are projected out. Each such truncation produces an (intentional, regularising) leak, and these leaks don't average to zero — they bias the trajectory.

9. Diagnosis — what went wrong

The verification has uncovered a real and surprising limitation:

K = 5 ECG basis is rich enough for the ground state of $H_\text{full}$ but too narrow for real-time evolution under $H_\text{evolve}$.

The ground-state search (imaginary time, gradient descent in the variational manifold) only requires the manifold to contain a state close to the true minimum. Real-time TDVP requires the manifold's tangent space at every point of the trajectory to match the Schrödinger flow direction $-iH|\psi\rangle$ projected onto that tangent space. When the C matrix has near-degenerate singular values — here $\text{cond}(C) \sim 3.5\times 10^{11}$ — SVD with $rcond = 10^{-4}$ projects out genuine motion directions along with the numerical noise, and the projection is not norm-preserving. The result is a TDVP step that conserves energy (because both the projected flow and the Hamiltonian gradient are real-valued) but slowly leaks norm and breaks the symmetries of the Hamiltonian.

Why the ground state was unaffected

  1. Imaginary-time TDVP uses $\dot z = -C^{-1} g$ (no factor of $i$), so the flow does not need to preserve the unitary inner product — only decrease energy. SVD truncation that breaks unitarity is harmless here.
  2. Step 1 also re-solves $u$ exactly via the generalised eigenvalue problem after each step (the “u resolve” in tdvp_step), which absorbs basis errors into the linear coefficients. No such resolve happens in real time.

Why energy stayed conserved

Even with the truncated $C^{-1}$, the projected flow is still $\dot z \propto C^{-1} \nabla E$. Following any descent direction of a smooth functional preserves the “flat” component (in this case, the constant-$E$ surface) up to roundoff. The bug is that the flow stays on the wrong constant-$E$ surface.

10. Recommended next steps

In increasing order of expected impact and effort:

optionchangecostexpected effect
A Set rt_cfg.enforce_norm = true 1 line Clamps norm to 1 after every step. Won't fix shape error or parity drift, but isolates them from the norm leak.
B Increase $K$ (e.g. $K = 8$ or $K = 10$) 1 CLI flag More basis lifts the C-matrix degeneracy; rcond truncation drops fewer real motion directions. Probably the real fix.
C Use Trotter splitting: rt_cfg.u_split_trotter = true 1 CLI flag Propagates $u$ analytically via $\exp(-iH dt)$ on fixed $\{A,B,R\}$, TDVP only handles $\{A,B,R\}$. Decouples the linear $u$ flow from the non-linear basis flow; might handle near-singular $C$ much better.
D Loosen $rcond$ (e.g. $10^{-6}$) 1 CLI flag Risky — could amplify numerical noise. Last resort.

My recommendation: do A + B together. Re-run with $K = 10$, $\text{enforce\_norm}=$ true, same $T = 2\pi$ but allow $dt = 10^{-3}$ to keep the wall-time manageable (RK4 truncation error stays well below machine precision at that $dt$).

Outcome. Step 4 has accomplished its purpose: cross-checking ECG real-time TDVP against two independent reference solvers has revealed that the K = 5 basis sufficient for the ground state is not sufficient for time evolution. Energy is conserved to machine precision, but norm leaks 53 % and the wavefunction loses its symmetries and recurrence within one HO period. The fix is to enrich the basis (and optionally enforce unitarity by post-correction); a follow-up run with $K = 10$ + enforce_norm is the next step before any larger-N or kicking dynamics can be trusted.
