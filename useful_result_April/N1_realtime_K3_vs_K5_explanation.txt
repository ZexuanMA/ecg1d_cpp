==============================================================================
Why does N=1 real-time TDVP behave WELL at K=3 but FAIL at K=5?
==============================================================================

Setup
-----
Both runs use the same evolution Hamiltonian H_evolve = T + V_ho (cosine
turned off for time evolution), the same time step dt = 1e-3, the same
final time T = 2*pi, the same SVD cutoff rcond = 1e-4, and the same
Tikhonov damping lambda_C = 1e-8. The only difference is the basis size
K = 3 vs K = 5.

  Seed (Step-1 ECG ground state of H_full = T + V_ho + kappa cos(2 k_L x))
  K=3 has E_full = 1.22583 (less converged)
  K=5 has E_full = 1.22264 (better converged; matches grid to 5 digits)

So K=5 is variationally STRICTLY BETTER for the ground state.  Yet for
the real-time evolution it is strictly WORSE.

==============================================================================
2. What goes wrong dynamically for K=5
==============================================================================

The exact dynamics under H_evolve is a coherent breathing of the seed at
frequency 2*omega:
    grid density peaks alternate between 0.43 (wide)  at t = 0, pi, 2pi
                                  and    0.68 (narrow) at t = pi/2, 3pi/2

K=3 reproduces this breathing exactly (within 1 percent of the grid).
K=5 does not breathe.  Its density peak grows monotonically from 0.41 at
t = 0 to 0.65 at t = 2*pi and stays there.  The variational state
collapses to a focused configuration and never recurses to the initial
seed shape.

Phase-by-phase comparison of the K=5 density peak vs grid:
                t          ECG peak     grid peak
              0.000          0.41          0.43
              1.257          0.54          0.68     <- ECG too wide
              3.142          0.63          0.43     <- ECG too narrow,
                                                       half-period inverted
              4.398          0.64          0.68
              6.283          0.65          0.43     <- no recurrence

So the K=5 result is not a small inaccuracy.  It is a qualitatively
different trajectory.

==============================================================================
3. Root cause: over-parameterised manifold + SVD truncation
==============================================================================

TDVP solves  i C dz = g  for the parameter velocity dz, where C is the
Gram matrix of tangent vectors (size d x d, d = number of variational
parameters).  If C has near-zero singular values they are truncated by
the rcond cutoff:
    C^+_r  =  sum_{sigma_i >= rcond * sigma_max}  (1 / sigma_i) v_i v_i^*

  K=3 (d ~ 12 variational parameters):  cond(C) ~ 1e2 - 1e3.
  Top 4 decades of singular values are easily inside rcond = 1e-4.
  Essentially nothing gets truncated.  SVD truncation is a no-op.
  The TDVP equation is solved faithfully, the projector P_T is
  effectively the full tangent projector, and the state breathes
  correctly.

  K=5 (d ~ 20):  cond(C) ~ 1e8 - 1e10.  The spectrum spans about 10
  decades.  rcond = 1e-4 keeps only the top 4 decades, dropping
  approximately HALF the singular directions.  The truncated projector
  P_{T_r} has a strictly smaller range than T_psi M.  Some of the
  dropped directions carry the parity-symmetric u-coefficient flow that
  is needed for proper breathing.  As a result:

    - <psi|psi> can change because |psi> escapes range(P_{T_r})
      (Q9: dN/dt = 2 Im <psi| P_{T_r} H |psi>, no longer zero).
    - The state acquires a parity-violating component because
      P_{T_r} does not commute with parity once its range is restricted.
    - The dynamics get stuck in a focused, non-breathing configuration.

This is the classic "over-parameterised variational manifold" pathology:
making the basis richer makes the unregularised manifold richer, but
since the Gram matrix becomes nearly singular, SVD truncation throws
away exactly the modes that the dynamics needs.  More variational
freedom thus DEGRADES the SVD-regularised TDVP, and a smaller manifold
that just barely covers the dynamics gives better real-time results.

==============================================================================
4. Why the ground state is BETTER at K=5 anyway
==============================================================================

Imaginary-time TDVP minimises <H_full> over the variational manifold.
A larger manifold contains a strictly lower minimum, so K=5 GS energy
must be <= K=3 GS energy.  Numerically:

    K=3  E_full = 1.22583
    K=5  E_full = 1.22264   (lower, matches grid to 5 digits)

The same SVD truncation that hurts the dynamics is BENIGN in
imaginary-time TDVP because the gradient g there points strictly
downhill in energy.  The component of g that lies in the truncated
subspace is also downhill, and dropping it just slows convergence in
those modes.  It does not introduce qualitative artefacts.

In real-time TDVP the gradient g is replaced by a UNITARY rotation
direction; truncating any component of it generates an unphysical drift
in the conserved quantities (norm, parity, structural <psi|H|psi>).
That is why the same truncation that is harmless for Step 1 is
catastrophic for Step 4 at K=5.

==============================================================================
5. How to fix K=5 without abandoning the bigger basis
==============================================================================

The single most effective lever is to lower the SVD cutoff so the
truncated projector keeps the modes the dynamics needs:

    --rcond 1e-6   keeps top 6 decades (drops ~1/4 of singular dirs)
    --rcond 1e-7   keeps top 7 decades (drops only the noisiest dirs)
    --rcond 1e-8   essentially no truncation; rely on lambda_C alone

Other knobs (in decreasing order of usefulness):

    --dt 1e-4              smaller integrator step; controls RK4 error
                           when |dz| spikes
    --lambda-C 1e-10       reduce Tikhonov damping in tandem
    --K 7                  bigger basis but combine with smaller rcond,
                           else the same problem grows

==============================================================================
6. Take-aways
==============================================================================

  - K=3 real-time dynamics are quantitatively faithful to the grid /
    DVR exact reference for the cosine-quench problem at N=1: peak
    heights agree to <1 %, L2 to <2e-3, breathing amplitude exact.

  - K=5 real-time dynamics qualitatively fail under default rcond:
    no breathing, half-period phase inversion, no recurrence at t=2pi.
    The failure shows up first as 53 % drift in raw <psi|H|psi> and
    parity violation at the 0.18 level.

  - The failure is NOT in the ground state (K=5 is converged) and NOT
    in the integrator (per-step normalised E drift is 3e-8, fine).
    It is in the SVD-regularised projector P_{T_r} losing the tangent
    directions needed for symmetric breathing.

  - K=3 happens to fall in the sweet spot where the variational
    manifold is just rich enough to carry the dynamics and small
    enough that SVD truncation does nothing.  K=5 is over-
    parameterised at rcond = 1e-4.

  - To use K=5 (or larger) for real-time, lower rcond.  Otherwise stick
    with K=3 for the dynamics study even though the ground state is
    less accurate.
