N=1, K=5 energy explanation
============================

Question:
Why does static_N1_K5.log show energy about 1.222642783, while
realtime_N1_K5.log shows energy about 0.583193830?

Conclusion:
Everything is consistent if the intended real-time evolution is without the
cosine potential.

The static run computes the Step-1 ground state of the full Hamiltonian:

    H_full = T + V_ho + V_cos

The final Step-1 energy components from static_N1_K5.log are:

    T       = 0.145331047104
    V_ho    = 0.437862782808
    V_cos   = 0.639448953224
    E_full  = 1.22264278314

The realtime run starts from the same Step-1 ECG wavefunction, loaded from:

    out/verify/step1_ecg_basis_N1_K5.csv

Then Step 4 turns off the cosine potential and evolves with:

    H_evolve = T + V_ho

Therefore the expected initial real-time energy is:

    E_no_cos = T + V_ho
             = 0.145331047104 + 0.437862782808
             = 0.583193829912

This matches realtime_N1_K5.log:

    [step4] E0=0.5831938299

The apparent decrease from 1.222642783 to 0.583193830 is not a dynamical
energy loss. It is the same initial wavefunction evaluated with a different
Hamiltonian after removing the cosine contribution:

    1.22264278314 - 0.639448953224 = 0.583193829916

Relevant code:

1. main_verification.cpp says Step 4 is real-time TDVP with cos potential
   turned off.

2. src/verify/step4_realtime_tdvp.cpp uses:

       HamiltonianTerms terms = HamiltonianTerms::kinetic_harmonic();

   This means only kinetic + harmonic terms are used in realtime Step 4.

3. src/verify/step4_reference_dynamics.cpp also builds:

       H_evolve = T + V_ho

   with no cosine term.

Important caveat:
The Hamiltonian choice and the energy value are correct for no-cos evolution.
However, the ECG realtime log shows norm drift because enforce_norm=off.
This is a numerical TDVP issue, not a sign that the no-cos energy value is
wrong.
