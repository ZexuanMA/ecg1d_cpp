Verification harness / mathematical process map

What main_verification.cpp Computes

This page describes the executable ecg1d_verify as a sequence of variational and reference problems. The code verifies an explicitly correlated Gaussian (ECG) ansatz by comparing it with finite-difference and sinc-DVR solvers at the level of energy, densities, momentum densities, moments, and real-time conservation laws.

1. Data Flow

The program is an end-to-end consistency test. It first constructs an ECG approximation to the ground state of the full static Hamiltonian. It then solves the same one-particle problem on two independent grids, compares all static observables, and finally performs a quench in which the cosine potential is removed and the ECG state is propagated in real time.

Parse flags

Select N,K, grids, time step, trace interval, snapshot count, and regularization parameters.

Step 1

Minimize the Rayleigh quotient over an ECG manifold for \(H_{\mathrm{full}}\).

Step 2

Diagonalize finite-difference and sinc-DVR Hamiltonian matrices on \([-x_{\max},x_{\max}]\).

Step 3

Compare energies, \(n(x)\), \(n(k)\), and wavefunction fidelity.

Step 4

Propagate with \(H_{\mathrm{evolve}}=T+V_{\mathrm{ho}}\), then compare ECG TDVP against spectral references.

Control dependency. Step 3 requires Step 1 and Step 2 in the same process. Step 4 needs a Step-1 basis; if Step 1 was skipped, it loads step1_ecg_basis_N*_K*.csv from the output directory.

2. Mathematical Model

In the constants used by the code, \(\hbar=m=\omega=1\), \(k_L=1/2\), and \(\kappa=1\). The static verification Hamiltonian is

\[ H_{\mathrm{full}} = \sum_{a=1}^{N} \left[ -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_a^2} + \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2x_a^2 + \kappa\cos(2k_Lx_a) \right]. \]

The real-time verification Hamiltonian switches off the cosine lattice:

\[ H_{\mathrm{evolve}} = \sum_{a=1}^{N} \left[ -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_a^2} + \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2x_a^2 \right]. \]

ECG Ansatz

Each ECG basis member is parameterized by a scalar coefficient \(u_i\), matrices \(A_i,B_i\), and a displacement vector \(R_i\). Up to the permutation symmetrization used in the code, the primitive Gaussian has the form

\[ \phi_i(x) = \exp\!\left[ -x^{T}(A_i+B_i)x + 2R_i^{T}B_ix - R_i^{T}B_iR_i \right], \] \[ |\Psi(z)\rangle = \sum_{i=1}^{K} u_i \sum_{P\in S_N} s(P)\,|\phi_i(Px)\rangle, \qquad z=(u,A,B,R). \]
In the current bosonic verification build, the permutation sign is \(s(P)=1\) for every \(P\in S_N\).

The reason the implementation can compute many quantities quickly is that every pairwise Gaussian integral is analytic. For a bra-ket pair \((i,j)\) and permutation \(P\), the product exponent is reduced to

\[ \phi_i^\ast(x)\,\phi_j(Px) = \exp\!\left( -x^T Kx + b^Tx + C \right), \qquad \mu=\frac{1}{2}K^{-1}b, \] \[ M_G(i,j,P) = \int_{\mathbb{R}^{N}} e^{-x^TKx+b^Tx+C}\,dx = \pi^{N/2}\det(K)^{-1/2} \exp\!\left(C+\frac{1}{4}b^TK^{-1}b\right). \]

Thus overlap and Hamiltonian matrices are sums over \(i,j,P\), for example

\[ S_{ij} = \sum_{P\in S_N} s(P)\,M_G(i,j,P), \qquad H_{ij} = \sum_{P\in S_N} s(P)\,M_G(i,j,P)\, \mathcal{K}_{H}(i,j,P). \]
\(\mathcal{K}_{H}\) denotes the analytic kernel generated by the selected Hamiltonian terms: kinetic, harmonic, contact, Gaussian, or cosine.

3. Step 1: ECG Imaginary-Time Ground State

Goal

step1_imag_time_ecg

Compute an ECG approximation to the lowest-energy state of \(H_{\mathrm{full}}\). The mathematical target is the constrained Rayleigh quotient

\[ E[z] = \frac{ \langle \Psi(z)|H_{\mathrm{full}}|\Psi(z)\rangle }{ \langle \Psi(z)|\Psi(z)\rangle }, \qquad z=(u,A,B,R). \]

The linear coefficients \(u\) are repeatedly reoptimized by the generalized eigenvalue problem

\[ Hc=ESc, \qquad E_0 = \min_{c\ne 0} \frac{c^\dagger Hc}{c^\dagger Sc}. \]
Initialization for N=1 The first Gaussian is the harmonic oscillator ground-state seed with \(A=1/2\), \(B=0\), \(R=0\); remaining basis members are random perturbations.
Initialization for N>=2 The code uses stochastic variational method (SVM) construction followed by stochastic refinement. Candidate basis functions are accepted when they lower the generalized eigenvalue without violating the energy floor.

Imaginary-Time TDVP Polish

After initialization, the nonlinear parameters are refined by a projected imaginary-time flow. The implementation assembles the variational metric \(C\) and Hamiltonian gradient \(g\) over the active coordinates \(\alpha \in \{u,B,R,A\}\). In compact form:

\[ T_\psi\mathcal{M} = \mathrm{span} \left\{ |\partial_\alpha\psi\rangle \right\}_{\alpha=1}^{d}, \qquad |\delta\psi\rangle = \sum_{\alpha=1}^{d} \delta z_\alpha |\partial_\alpha\psi\rangle . \] \[ C_{\alpha\beta} = \langle\partial_\alpha\psi| \partial_\beta\psi\rangle, \qquad g_\alpha = \frac{\partial}{\partial z_\alpha^\ast} \langle\psi|H_{\mathrm{full}}|\psi\rangle . \] \[ C^\dagger\Delta z=-g \qquad \text{(imaginary-time descent direction used by Step 1).} \]

The solve is regularized by \(C\mapsto C+\lambda_C I\) and by an SVD pseudo-inverse. A line search accepts only energy-decreasing moves; then \(u\) is re-solved from \(Hc=ESc\).

4. Step 2: Independent Reference Ground States

Goal

step2_reference_gs

Construct two independent discretizations of the same one-particle Hamiltonian and diagonalize them. Because no inter-particle interaction is present in this verification problem, the total reference energy is simply

\[ H_{\mathrm{sp}}\psi_0 = E_{\mathrm{sp}}\psi_0, \qquad E_{\mathrm{ref}}(N) = N E_{\mathrm{sp}} . \]
Finite difference On a uniform grid with spacing \(\Delta x\), \(T_{jj}=1/(m\Delta x^2)\) and \(T_{j,j\pm1}=-1/(2m\Delta x^2)\).
Sinc-DVR The kinetic matrix is dense: \(T_{ii}=\pi^2/(6m\Delta x^2)\), \(T_{ij}=(-1)^{i-j}/[m\Delta x^2(i-j)^2]\).

Both references add the same diagonal potential \(V(x)=x^2/2+\kappa\cos(2k_Lx)\), normalize the eigenvector by \(\sum_j|\psi_j|^2\Delta x=1\), and compute

\[ n(x_j)=|\psi_j|^2, \qquad \widetilde{\psi}(k) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \sum_j \psi_j e^{-ikx_j}\Delta x, \qquad n(k)=|\widetilde{\psi}(k)|^2 . \]

5. Step 3: Static Cross-Check

Goal

step3_cross_check

Compare the ECG, finite-difference, and sinc-DVR solutions as probability measures and, for N=1, as complex wavefunctions. The code first interpolates quantities onto common grids and then evaluates pairwise diagnostics.

Energy error \(\Delta E=E_a-E_b\), \(\Delta E/E_b\).
Density distance \(\|n_a-n_b\|_2 = [\int (n_a-n_b)^2\,dx]^{1/2}\).
Bhattacharyya overlap \(B(n_a,n_b)=\int\sqrt{n_a(x)n_b(x)}\,dx\).
\[ F(\psi_a,\psi_b) = \frac{ |\langle\psi_a|\psi_b\rangle|^2 }{ \|\psi_a\|^2\|\psi_b\|^2 }. \]
This wavefunction fidelity is computed only when complex wavefunctions are available, mainly for \(N=1\).

The pair list is ECG_vs_grid, ECG_vs_dvr, and grid_vs_dvr. The resulting CSV is step3_crosscheck_N*.csv.

6. Step 4: Real-Time TDVP After a Quench

Goal

step4_realtime_tdvp

Start from the Step-1 ECG ground state of \(H_{\mathrm{full}}\), then propagate using \(H_{\mathrm{evolve}}=T+V_{\mathrm{ho}}\). This is a quench:

\[ |\psi(0)\rangle \approx \operatorname*{arg\,min}_{|\psi\rangle\in\mathcal{M}} \frac{\langle\psi|H_{\mathrm{full}}|\psi\rangle} {\langle\psi|\psi\rangle}, \qquad i\partial_t|\psi(t)\rangle = H_{\mathrm{evolve}}|\psi(t)\rangle . \]
The cosine term \(V_{\mathrm{kick}}=\kappa\cos(2k_Lx)\) is present in the preparation Hamiltonian and absent in the propagation Hamiltonian.

ECG TDVP Evolution

For real time, the Dirac-Frenkel equations are written in the variational coordinates as

\[ |\dot\psi\rangle = \sum_{\beta=1}^{d} \dot z_\beta|\partial_\beta\psi\rangle, \qquad |R\rangle = i|\dot\psi\rangle - H_{\mathrm{evolve}}|\psi\rangle . \] \[ \min_{\dot z}\|R\|^2 \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \langle\partial_\alpha\psi|R\rangle=0 \quad(\alpha=1,\dots,d). \] \[ i\sum_{\beta=1}^{d} \langle\partial_\alpha\psi|\partial_\beta\psi\rangle \dot z_\beta = \langle\partial_\alpha\psi| H_{\mathrm{evolve}}|\psi\rangle . \] \[ i\,C_{\alpha\beta}\dot z_\beta=g_\alpha, \qquad C_{\alpha\beta} = \langle\partial_\alpha\psi|\partial_\beta\psi\rangle, \qquad g_\alpha = \langle\partial_\alpha\psi|H_{\mathrm{evolve}}|\psi\rangle . \]

The code solves this as \(\dot z=-iC^{+}g\) with an SVD pseudo-inverse. The command-line options --lambda-C, --rcond, --wiener, and --no-wiener determine how the nearly singular variational metric is regularized.

\[ T = \begin{bmatrix} |\partial_1\psi\rangle& |\partial_2\psi\rangle& \cdots& |\partial_d\psi\rangle \end{bmatrix}, \qquad P_T = T(T^\dagger T)^+T^\dagger . \] \[ |\dot\psi\rangle_{\mathrm{TDVP}} = -iP_T H_{\mathrm{evolve}}|\psi\rangle, \qquad \epsilon(t) = \|(1-P_T)H_{\mathrm{evolve}}|\psi(t)\rangle\|. \]
This is the same projected-Schrodinger picture used in past_md/TDVP_STEP4_CH9_QA.md: TDVP keeps only the part of the exact velocity that lies in the ECG tangent space.
Numerical optionMathematical effect
lambda_C Tikhonov shift: \(C\mapsto C+\lambda_C I\).
rcond Defines the singular-value scale \(\sigma_{\mathrm{cut}}=\mathrm{rcond}\,\sigma_{\max}\).
--wiener Smooth pseudo-inverse: \(\sigma^+=\sigma/(\sigma^2+\sigma_{\mathrm{cut}}^2)\).
--no-wiener Hard truncation: \(\sigma^+=1/\sigma\) if \(\sigma>\sigma_{\mathrm{cut}}\), otherwise \(0\).

The default integrator used through the verification wrapper is fixed-step RK4. The evolution is split into legs whose endpoints are the requested snapshot times. At each trace sample the code records

\[ E(t) = \frac{\langle\psi(t)|H_{\mathrm{evolve}}|\psi(t)\rangle} {\langle\psi(t)|\psi(t)\rangle}, \qquad N(t)=\langle\psi(t)|\psi(t)\rangle . \] \[ \langle X\rangle,\quad \langle P\rangle,\quad \langle X^2\rangle,\quad \langle P^2\rangle, \qquad F_0(t) = \frac{ |\langle\psi(0)|\psi(t)\rangle|^2 }{ \|\psi(0)\|^2\|\psi(t)\|^2 }. \]

Reference Dynamics for N=1

The reference real-time solvers are implemented only for one particle. They diagonalize the finite-difference and sinc-DVR versions of \(H_{\mathrm{evolve}}\), evaluate the ECG initial state on the reference grid, and propagate spectrally:

\[ H_{\mathrm{ref}}U=U\Lambda, \qquad c=U^T\psi_0, \qquad \psi(t) = U\,\mathrm{diag} \left(e^{-i\lambda_n t}\right)c . \]

Dynamic Cross-Check

At each snapshot time the program compares ECG, grid, and DVR trajectories. The dynamic diagnostics extend Step 3 by including moment errors:

\[ \Delta E(t),\quad |\Delta x(t)|,\quad |\Delta p(t)|,\quad |\Delta x^2(t)|,\quad |\Delta p^2(t)|, \] \[ \|n_a(\cdot,t)-n_b(\cdot,t)\|_2, \qquad B(t) = \int \sqrt{n_a(x,t)n_b(x,t)}\,dx . \]
Conservation test. Since \(H_{\mathrm{evolve}}\) is time independent, the exact dynamics conserves energy. The ECG TDVP trace therefore reports \(\max_t|E(t)-E(0)|/|E(0)|\) as the main structural check, and also checks the raw quantity \(\langle\psi|H|\psi\rangle=E(t)\|\psi(t)\|^2\).

One-Line Interpretation

Mathematically, the executable asks whether the ECG variational manifold gives the same ground-state and post-quench observables as two grid-based discretizations:

\[ |\Psi_{\mathrm{ECG}}\rangle \xrightarrow[\text{imaginary-time TDVP}]{H_{\mathrm{full}}} |\Psi_0\rangle \xrightarrow[\text{real-time TDVP}]{H_{\mathrm{evolve}}} |\Psi(t)\rangle . \] \[ \bigl\{ E,\ n(x),\ n(k),\ F,\ \langle x^q p^r\rangle \bigr\}_{\mathrm{ECG}} \approx \bigl\{\cdot\bigr\}_{\mathrm{FD}} \approx \bigl\{\cdot\bigr\}_{\mathrm{DVR}}. \]
